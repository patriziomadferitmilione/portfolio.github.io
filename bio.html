My Journey

My Journey

My journey to Web Development was definitely a non-linear one.

Allow me to explain.

Let's go back to the very beginning:
I come from a working class family,
we moved houses a few times when I was a kid,
but I had a pretty normal and happy childwood.

I was a very lively child, doing lots of activities.
Through a friend of my dad's, I started acting and modelling at 2, which was quite the exciting hobby for a baby.
I started playing soccer at 4,
then playing piano at 6.

I grew up in a small town in the Alps, near Turin (Italy).
During my high school years, I gave more time and attention to my social life, rather than my studies; I was a tiny bit rebellious, and not interested in school at all.

I self taught myself to play the guitar at 16, then started writing songs at 17, I was obsessed with becoming a Rock 'n' Roll star. I would spend my days out with friends or playing my favourite tunes in my room.

Around the same time, I developed my passion for computers.
Back in 2008/2009, buying online wasn't nearly as popular as it is now, so I had access to a whole lot of cool stuff for free.

I hacked my first iPhone to download apps for free and to add weird features,
I started a website using BlogSpot and dabbled with Wordpress quite a bit.
I also spent hours reading forum posts on how to install cheats in my videogames, change the soundtracks and so on.

At 19, tired of wasting my time, I left school, worked at my dad's pizzeria until the summer to save a bit of money (I started working for him at 13, during school holidays and weekends), then I moved to London, with the dream of making it with my music.

I worked mainly as a waiter while following my dream and I managed to join a few bands, go on tour twice, play more than 100 hours on stage, in the same venues and pubs where my idols used to play at my age, write more songs and, kind of, live the dream.

After 3 years of this intense lifestyle, I had some sort of nervous breakdown, due to stress, lack of sleep and excessive partying, so I made my way back home to get some rest.

I shortly stayed at my mom's, then moved to Turin where I kept working in restaurants and working on my music. I formed a band, joined a few contests, played in a few venues as well as on the street, and released three singles.

In 2018, I decided it was time to leave again.
I moved to Sydney, Australia, where I spent a year, repeating the pattern of working in the hospitality industry while creating bands and writing music. I played the Hard Rock Cafe many times, busked on the beach, had my share of adventures and made my way home once again.

After a brief period at home, I moved to London again, worked for Gordon Ramsay, played my music in pubs as well as on the streets, then Covid happened. After about 6 months of getting by in the big city without live music, I came to terms with my age and dreams, and decided to stop chasing the Rockstar dream, once and for all.

Enriched from all my adventures and experiences, having worked about 20 different jobs while living in 3 cities around the world, I decided it was time to set new goals for myself. The pandemic with its lockdowns made me realize how quickly the world is changing, and that I had to do something if I wanted to hop on board of this fast moving train.

I decided that my career would be in IT. I needed a field in which I could be creative, constantly learn new things and be able to take part in the technological revolution we've been living for the past few decades. I liked the idea of having a job in demand, since I had spent the last 8 years chasing Rock 'n' Roll (some say it died long ago...). I also wanted the chance to self teach myself all the skills needed to make this career shift. After an extensive research, I decided for Web Development. I love the idea of being able to create Websites, Apps and Software.

I've studied it through online resources (Udemy, Youtube and whatnot) from June 2021 to January 2022, an average of 10 hours per week, while working full-time in a warehouse. Then, towards the end of January, I landed a Software Engineer internship in Turin. It was an intense 3 months full of learning.

Life-changing.

In may 2022 I took on a new challenge, as a Blockchain Developer. I started things off with the development of an Application that communicates with a Blockchain to certify the completion of courses.

Unfortunately, things didn't work out as I was the only developer on the project, and being junior and self-taught, I wanted to have experienced people around me to learn from.

So I got a job at a software house to keep gaining new skills. We build a Project Management Software for enterprises and small businesses through a low code platform.

I work remotely and I couldn't be happier.
